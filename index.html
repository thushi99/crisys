Blockchain Based Criminal Information Management System in Sri Lanka

A Decentralized criminal Record management system for Sri Lankan Law Enforcement Agencies to increase the Security and Privacy

Scope

Background & Literature Survey

The blockchain was first made public as the underlying technology for Bitcoin, but it has now moved beyond its financial roots to become a focus of investigation for researchers and inventors. One such area where blockchain technology has enormous potential is the administration of criminal information. Its potential influence goes far beyond conventional financial applications, and its adoption is anticipated to have a large positive impact on the criminal justice system.

In Sri Lanka, the adoption of a blockchain-based criminal information management system could significantly improve data accuracy and accessibility. A decentralized ledger would ensure that records are tamper-proof, reducing the risk of corruption or unauthorized alterations. Additionally, the transparent nature of blockchain would promote trust among law enforcement agencies, legal authorities, and the general public. However, following drawbacks were observed,

  • Lack of Individual Control
  • Scattered Criminal Records
  • Interoperability Challenges
  • Inefficient, Complex Data Sharing
  • Data Fragmentation
Research Gap / Novelty

Proposed Blockchain based criminal information management system stand apart from other systems with following features,

  • Smart contracts can be used to automate certain tasks related to criminal information management
  • Multi-factor Authentication will enhanced the security and privacy of criminal records.
  • IPFS Secure file management will provide the criminmal evidence more accuratly and High Security.
  • Chain Of Custody will ensure all the logs are Securely collected and monitored within the System.
  • Trust and reliability: Blockchain's immutability ensures that once data is recorded, it cannot be altered or deleted without proper authorization.
  • Automation and efficiency: Smart contracts can be programmed to automate routine tasks, such as record updates, notifications, and data verification.

Research Problems

Centralized Criminal Records system addresses following research problems

  • Security and privacy: How can blockchain-based criminal information management systems be designed to ensure the security and privacy of sensitive criminal data?
  • Scalability and performance: How can blockchain-based criminal information management systems be scaled to handle large amounts of data and transactions without sacrificing performance?
  • Interoperability: How can blockchain-based criminal information management systems be made interoperable with existing criminal justice systems?
  • Auditing and accountability: How can blockchain-based criminal information management systems be audited to ensure that they are used fairly and ethically?
  • Public acceptance: How can public acceptance of blockchain-based criminal information management systems be increased?

Objectives

Main Objective
  • User Secure Data Management: Managing criminal records securely and dependably is one of the main sub-objectives of a blockchain-based criminal information management system.
  • Efficient Data Sharing
  • Ensuring Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability
  • Improved Case Management

Methodology

Methodology implements following four components,

  • An agile paradigm was used for the process of developing software
  • For Multi-factor authentication using Complexity of the OTP code and the facial recognization.
  • Secure File Management system using Interplanetary File System.
  • Chain Of Custody process with Secure Encryption
  • The distributed ledger concept ensures that an exact copy of the information is distributed to all parties
  • Blockchain platform : Ganache Private Blockchain for Criminal System.
